About Me

I am a Research Scientist at Spotify, where my work focuses on bridging the gap between generative language models and search and recommendation tasks. Prior to joining Spotify, I was a Senior Machine Learning Scientist at Tripadvisor, where I developed search models for discovering experiences on Viator.

I hold a PhD in Machine Learning from the University of Glasgow. My doctoral research, supervised by Prof. Craig Macdonald and Prof. Iadh Ounis, focused on building effective and efficient transformer models for sequential recommendation. My broader research interests lie at the intersection of recommender systems, information retrieval, and deep learning.

Before my academic transition, I spent 12 years as a software engineer, including a tenure as a Senior Software Engineer at Amazon.com. During that time, I focused on recommendations, personalization, search, and online advertising, gaining extensive hands-on experience with production-scale systems.

In addition to my core research, I have supervised Master's projects at the University of Edinburgh and taught machine learning and data analysis courses in Russian. When I'm not at my desk, I enjoy driving around the Scottish Highlands, studying languages, and reading fantasy books.
